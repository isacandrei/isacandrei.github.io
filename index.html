Getting here

Travel

Flying

The best way to get to Timisoara from Central, South and Western Europe is flying with a layover in Germany (Munich/Frankfurth). Budapest and Belgrade are other options, but they are not the best. We'll explain below each option.

Possible Options:

  1. Timisoara

    Tested throught the years, this is by far the most convienent, fastest and nowadays even cheapest way of getting to Timisoara. With a fast security check in Zurich/Geneva, a layover of 1-1.5h just enough for coffee, and 2.5h of flying, you'll be in 4-5h from airport to airport.

    Google Flights:

    • Zurich - Timisoara

    • Geneva - Timisoara

    • If you want to visit Romania and do the proposed road trip one option is to fly to Timisoara and fly back directly from Bucharest. Zurich - Timisoara, Bucharest - Zurich

  2. Budapest

    Budapest has a direct flight from Zurich and Basel, but it takes 4h from the Airport to Timisoara and you need to book a seat in a minivan (which means that the minivan will wait for other passangers to land as well, which may lead to 2-3h wait in the airport in Budapest).

  3. Belgrade

    Belgrade has a direct flight from Zurich, but it is not in EU which means that you might spend more time at the borders control and need to check the conditions for taking a rented car from Belgrade outside of Serbia. Moreover, there is no highway from Serbia to Timisoara, so it's usally slower than what Google maps says.

Driving

Driving from Zurich to Timisoara is also throughly tested (last time was in September :) ). It's a nice trip to do as you can be on the highway from Zurich all the way to Timisoara or you can take a picturesque route through the Austrian Alps. We recommend to stop in Vienna on the way (it's half way).

We suggest the that if you haven't been to Timisoara yet, it's better to stay in the city center so you can also visit the city before or after the wedding. Full road trip

This is a proposal for an itinerary. Of course we can adjust it and if you're interested in joing please let us know in the RSVP and we'll add you to a whatsapp group.

Hunedoara

  • Wake up late, enjoy some coffee and lunch in Timisoara.
  • Optional city guided tour.
  • Drive to Hunedoara.
  • Visit Corvinilor's Medieval Castle on the way to the national park.
  • Dinner and Sleep in Hunedoara in some nice hotel.

Retezat National Park

Close to where Andrei comes from lies the most beatiful mountain range in Romania.

  • Option 1) Hike to Retezat Peak and Bucura Lake.
  • Option 2) Visit Sarmisegetusa ruins
  • Sleep in a mountain hut or mountain airbnb cabin.

Sibiu Medieval Town

One of the nicest medieval towns in Romania with a lot of habsburg influence.

  • Hike down from Retezat or do a shorter nature walk in the national park.
  • Drive to Sibiu.
  • Visit the city.
  • Sleep in a nice bohemiam hotel near Sibiu.

Central Carpathian Mountains

Rich both in natural beauty and history, this part of the country has much to offer.

  • Drive on the famous TopGear Transfagarasean mountain pass roads.
  • Reach Balea Lake.
  • Hike or enjoy activitities at the lake. Maybe you get to see a bear :P
  • Enjoy some fresh Romanian white cheese and shepards traditional recipies.
  • Sleep in the mountain cabin or in an airbnb cabin.

Brasov and Peles Castle

A mix of the medieval town of Brasov and the burgeis style of royal Peles Castle.

  • Drive to Brasov and visit the old town.
  • Enjoy lunch in the traditional restaurants Brasov
  • There might be some music festivals going on in Brasov.
  • Visit Peles Castle ( much nicer than Dracula's castle).
  • Sleep in a mountain airbnb.

Bucharest

Leaving the Carpathian mountain range to reach the vibrant Romanian capital of Bucharest.

  • Drive to Bucharest.
  • Visit the city and enjoy the busy style of Bucharest after the tranquil medieval towns.
  • Visit the Parliament house which is the second largest building in the world after the Pentagon.
  • Enjoy the nightlife in Bucharest.
  • Fly directly from Bucharest the next day or drive back to Timisoara.
